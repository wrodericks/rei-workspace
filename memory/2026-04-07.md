## FX Trading Strategy Discussion

Warren is exploring a small FX trading experiment ("a few thousand dollars as a toy"). Key points:

### Warren's Core Idea (Mean Reversion + Carry)
- Start with CAD
- When CAD is historically **strong** vs a foreign currency (near top of its long-run range) → convert to that currency
- Hold in an interest-bearing account, earning foreign deposit rates while waiting
- Convert back to CAD when it reverts toward the mean
- "Historical" = ~40 years, excluding periods with permanent valuation shifts due to sovereign crises
- **No leverage** — own money only
- Current interest: CAD/JPY specifically — CAD is nearing historic strength vs JPY even while weak vs USD

### Other FX Strategies Discussed (Warren wants to remember these)
1. **Mean Reversion** — trade currencies back toward long-run fair value; Warren's current idea
2. **Carry Trade** — borrow in low-rate currency (JPY, CHF), hold high-rate currency; earn rate differential; risk: violent unwinds in risk-off environments
3. **Trend Following / Momentum** — FX trends persist over weeks/months due to macro divergence; if strengthening for 3 months, stay long
4. **PPP Trading** — use inflation-adjusted fair value (Purchasing Power Parity) as anchor; Big Mac Index is a rough proxy; very long timeframes
5. **Central Bank Divergence** — trade currencies where central banks are moving in opposite directions (one hiking, one cutting)
